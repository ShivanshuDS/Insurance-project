💡 How Insurance Costs Are Calculated

🎂
Age Factor

Older individuals pay higher premiums. Medical needs rise significantly after 50, increasing insurer risk substantially.

🚬
Smoking Status

Smokers pay up to 3× more than non-smokers. Tobacco use is the single biggest driver of premium increases.

⚖️
BMI Impact

Higher BMI means greater health risk. A BMI above 30 (obese range) can significantly raise premiums.

👨‍👩‍👧
Dependants

Each additional child adds a modest premium increase. Family plans cost more than individual plans.

🗺️
Region

Healthcare costs vary by US region. The Southeast tends to have higher costs due to lifestyle factors.

🤖
ML Model

Trained on 1,338 real insurance records using a regression model to estimate costs from your profile.

✅ Tips to Reduce Your Cost

🚭
Quit smoking — smokers pay $23,000+ annually vs ~$8,000 for non-smokers. The biggest single change you can make.
⚖️
Maintain a healthy BMI (18.5–24.9). Even a modest weight reduction reduces risk factors and can lower premiums.
🏃
Exercise regularly. Active lifestyles reduce chronic disease risk, which insurers factor into long-term pricing models.
📋
Choose a higher-deductible plan if healthy — lower monthly premiums in exchange for higher out-of-pocket costs when needed.
🔍
Compare plans annually during open enrollment. Old plans often miss better, cheaper options that have since appeared.
